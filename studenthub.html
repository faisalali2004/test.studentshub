Pakistan has a diverse range of universities and higher education institutions offering programs in fields such as engineering, medicine, business, law, social sciences, and humanities. Some of the top-ranked universities include Quaid-i-Azam University, LUMS, NUST, UET, and Karachi University. Specialized institutions include the Aga Khan University, Lahore School of Economics, and Pakistan Institute of Development Economics. The education sector in Pakistan has seen reforms in recent years, focusing on improving quality, access, and innovation. However, challenges still exist, such as limited resources, inadequate infrastructure, and low enrollment rates for women and marginalized communities.

Pakistan university entry tests are a common requirement for admission to undergraduate programs at many universities in the country. These tests typically assess a student's knowledge and skills in subjects such as mathematics, English, and science. Depending on the university and program, the entry test may also include questions on specific subjects relevant to the field of study. The tests are usually conducted by universities or by independent testing services authorized by the Higher Education Commission. The results of the entry test are an important factor in determining a student's eligibility for admission to a particular program, along with their academic record and other criteria such as interviews or essays. Students must prepare well for these tests in order to secure admission to their desired university and program.

Pakistan's university admission schedule typically begins after the announcement of intermediate level examination results in May-June. The admission process involves submitting an application form and academic records. The admission is based on merit, which considers academic performance and the university entry test where applicable. Successful candidates must complete the enrollment process by paying fees and submitting additional documents. Admission dates and deadlines vary by university and program.

The aggregate system is commonly used by universities in Pakistan to evaluate eligibility for admission to undergraduate programs. It involves combining a student's scores in various components, such as intermediate level exams and university entry tests, into a single aggregate score. The system aims to provide a fair and transparent method for ranking students and selecting the most qualified candidates for admission.
